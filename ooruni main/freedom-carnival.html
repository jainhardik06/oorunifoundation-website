Freedom Carnival


Freedom Carnival is an ad hoc celebration organized by Ooruni Foundation under the “Give Their World” Project as an annual event for kids with learning disabilities and developmental disabilities, including Autism Spectrum Disorder, Down syndrome, Asperger syndrome, and Cerebral Palsy.

The importance of the event is to make the special kids explore their possibilities in a fun-filled environment, where infrastructure as per their needs is provided in the form of games to play, moves to express, and the freedom to be themselves.

It is high time to create an understanding of their deficiency issues and mobilize support for the dignity, rights, and well-being of persons with these deficiencies. It also seeks to increase awareness of the benefits of integrating persons with special abilities and special needs in every aspect of social, economic, and cultural life. This annual event is always celebrated on India’s Independence Day, on 15th August every year since 2017, for special kids by providing them and their parents with a vibrant experience. It is an endeavor to bring a new perspective to society to accept, support, and strengthen those blessed gifts of the Almighty.

2019 Freedom Carnival

Venue: MNM Jain Engineering College, Thoraipakkam, Chennai.

Attendance: 1500 kids accompanied by 650 parents and caretakers.

Highlights:

  • A book titled “Give Their World” was released, commemorating the legacy and success of Freedom Carnival.
  • The event focused on the overall development and balancing of physical, mental, and emotional well-being for all participants.
  • Special Child Assistance Network (SCAN), a group of parents with special needs children, facilitated discussions and shared experiences.
  • Expert talks by professionals, including Mrs. RajaRajeshwari, Consultant Physiotherapist, and Mr. Sriram, a special parent, inspired the audience.
  • A Parkour space was introduced to demonstrate techniques for overcoming physical and mental obstacles.
  • Tarang, a Self-Help Group of differently-abled individuals, prepared and served mid-morning snacks, inspiring attendees.
  • Anybody Can Dance (ABCD) sessions, conducted by G. Kavya Krithika, encouraged participants to express themselves through dance.
  • Science-based learning for daily activities was conducted by Arivarasan V., Program Director of Parikshan Charitable Trust.
  • Preenu Ashok, a creative therapist, used art as an intervention to address behavioral and psychological issues.
  • A Puppetry workshop and show by Isai Prakash entertained and captivated the audience.
  • The event concluded with a thank-you note by Mr. Arumugam, Trustee of Ooruni Foundation, ensuring parents of continued support and promising even better events in the future.
